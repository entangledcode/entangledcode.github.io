The Language

QRL — Quantum Relational Language

A relations-first approach to quantum computing where entanglement is a first-class citizen, not a derived concept. The key formal contribution: causal structure as a type-level primitive. A program that type-checks as Switch(d) is provably causally nonseparable — a result no existing quantum programming language can state.

Implements five foundational papers: process matrices (Oreshkov 2012), causal nonseparability witnesses (Araújo 2015), quantum causal models (Allen 2017), loophole-free Bell tests (Hensen 2015), and categorical quantum mechanics (Coecke & Kissinger 2017). Hardware-verified on Quandela's qpu:belenos photonic QPU (S = 2.61 ± 0.08).

~13,600 lines 896 tests passing 24 modules Paper in preparation — QPL 2027
