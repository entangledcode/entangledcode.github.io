The Quantum Relational Language

QRL is a quantum programming language built from first principles of information theory and relational physics. Where conventional quantum computing starts with classical bits and applies gates, QRL starts with quantum relations — correlations between systems that may be entangled, causal, or indefinite.

Relations-first, not gates-first
Entanglement is defined directly as a QuantumRelation — a correlation between two systems. No circuit decomposition, no basis rotation, no gate sequences. The physics is the program.
Causal structure as a first-class concept
QRL encodes causal relationships between quantum systems explicitly. Interventions, d-separation, and quantum do-calculus are built into the language — not bolted on as classical post-processing.
Compiles directly to photonic hardware
QRL programs compile to MBQC measurement patterns and then to Perceval circuits — runnable on Quandela photonic QPUs. Bell states have been verified on qpu:belenos, Quandela's 12-qubit flagship QPU.
Peer-reviewed and open source
QRL was presented at QPL 2026 (Quantum Physics and Logic, Amsterdam). The implementation — ~9,500 lines, 595 tests — is open source on GitHub and archived on Zenodo with a permanent DOI.
